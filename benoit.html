Global Warming 1855-2010

For this project, we wanted to tell stories of different time periods and their impact on the warming of our Earth. Later, we will look at how the Second Industrial Revolution impacted major cities who did and did not take part in the industrialization as well as US States impacted by Factory Farming.

First, I want to present to you a D3 interactive graph showing both Global and Country level changes in temperature.

For this project, we chose three counrties from each Hemisphere to represent in this graph. The green lines (United States, Germany, and Sweden) are Northern Hemisphere countries. The blue lines (Venezuela, Democratic Congo, and Indonesia) are near the equator. The red lines (Argentina, New Zealand, and South Africa) are part of the Southern Hemisphere.

The pink line is the average temperature of both land and ocean around the Globe

A D3 Interactive Chart

As we can see, regardless of where a country is located on the earth, average temperature is rising.

The noticable 5 year increases go as follows:

1. 1870-1875 : an increase of 0.10˚C

2. 1890-1895 : +0.12˚C

3. 1935-1940 : +0.14˚C

4. 1975-1980 : +0.14˚C

5. 1990-1995 : +0.12˚C

6. 1995-2000 : +0.10˚C

There are plenty of increases over 0.05˚C, the ones mentioned are simply the most substatial. The majority of these increases happen after 1910.

There are also big decreases in average temperature as well. Only there are fewer. They are as follows:

1. 1875-1880 : a decrease of 0.12˚C

2. 1940-1945 : -0.10˚C
